Gatlinburg Calendar of Events 2025

Nestled amidst the breathtaking Smoky Mountains, Gatlinburg is a vibrant city that offers a year-round calendar of exciting events. From thrilling festivals to captivating performances, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in this enchanting destination. Here’s a comprehensive guide to the most anticipated events of 2025 in Gatlinburg:

January

  • Winterfest: (January 1-February 28) Embrace the winter wonderland with ice skating, snow tubing, and festive activities at Ober Gatlinburg.
  • Gatlinburg Winterfest Craft Show: (January 7-9) Discover unique handmade crafts from local artisans at this indoor show held at the Gatlinburg Convention Center.

February

  • Valentine’s Day in Gatlinburg: (February 14) Celebrate love with romantic dining experiences, special events, and live music throughout the city.
  • Great Smoky Mountains Winterfest: (February 19-20) Experience the magic of winter with live music, craft vendors, and snow-themed activities at the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.

March

  • St. Patrick’s Day Parade and Festival: (March 17) Don your green attire and join the lively parade and festival featuring Irish music, dance, and food.
  • Gatlinburg Spring Arts & Crafts Show: (March 18-20) Browse an array of handmade arts and crafts from talented artisans at this outdoor show held at the Gatlinburg Convention Center.

April

  • Bloomin’ Barbeque & Bluegrass Festival: (April 21-23) Savor delicious barbecue and enjoy live bluegrass music at this three-day festival held at the Gatlinburg Convention Center.
  • Gatlinburg Wildflower Pilgrimage: (April 24-May 7) Explore the vibrant wildflowers of the Great Smoky Mountains during this guided tour.

May

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park BioBlitz: (May 5-7) Join scientists and naturalists to identify and document the incredible biodiversity of the national park.
  • Gatlinburg Spring Rod Run: (May 12-15) Admire classic cars and hot rods as they cruise through the streets of Gatlinburg.

June

  • Great Smoky Mountains Heritage Center Summer Solstice Festival: (June 20-21) Celebrate the longest day of the year with live music, storytelling, and traditional crafts.
  • Gatlinburg Summer Craftsmen’s Fair: (June 23-July 2) Discover the work of skilled artisans from across the country at this indoor and outdoor show.

July

  • Gatlinburg Fourth of July Celebration: (July 4) Celebrate Independence Day with a spectacular fireworks display over the Gatlinburg Convention Center.
  • Gatlinburg Summerfest: (July 14-August 6) Enjoy live music, carnival rides, and food vendors at this lively festival held at the Gatlinburg Convention Center.

August

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park Great American Campout: (August 11-13) Camp under the stars and participate in ranger-led activities at the national park.
  • Gatlinburg Craftsmen’s Fair: (August 18-20) Find unique handmade crafts and souvenirs at this outdoor show held at the Gatlinburg Convention Center.

September

  • Oktoberfest in Gatlinburg: (September 15-October 2) Raise a stein and enjoy German food, beer, and live music at this annual celebration.
  • Gatlinburg Fall Craftsmen’s Fair: (September 22-October 2) Browse an array of handmade crafts and fall-themed items at this outdoor show.

October

  • Halloween in Gatlinburg: (October 31) Dress up in your best costumes and join the spooky festivities throughout the city, including trick-or-treating and costume contests.
  • Gatlinburg Great Pumpkin LumiNights: (October 1-31) Marvel at thousands of illuminated pumpkins carved into intricate designs at Ripley’s Aquarium of the Smokies.

November

  • Veterans Day Parade and Celebration: (November 11) Honor our veterans with a parade and patriotic ceremony.
  • Gatlinburg Christmas Arts & Crafts Show: (November 11-13) Find unique handmade Christmas gifts and decorations at this indoor show.

December

  • Winter Magic in Gatlinburg: (November 24-February 28) Experience the festive spirit with twinkling lights, holiday decorations, and special events throughout the city.
  • Gatlinburg Fantasy of Lights Christmas Parade: (December 3) Witness a spectacular parade featuring floats, marching bands, and Santa Claus.
  • New Year’s Eve in Gatlinburg: (December 31) Ring in the new year with a fireworks display over the Gatlinburg Convention Center and live music throughout the city.

Additional Tips for Planning Your Trip

  • Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak season.
  • Purchase tickets for popular events ahead of time.
  • Check the event website for detailed schedules and information.
  • Allow ample time for parking and transportation.
  • Consider purchasing a Gatlinburg City Pass for discounted admission to attractions and activities.
  • Dress comfortably and bring layers for changing weather conditions.
  • Take advantage of the free Gatlinburg Trolley system for convenient transportation around the city.

Whether you’re seeking adventure, culture, or festive fun, Gatlinburg’s calendar of events 2025 offers something to ignite your spirit. Embrace the vibrant atmosphere and create unforgettable memories in this enchanting mountain destination.
