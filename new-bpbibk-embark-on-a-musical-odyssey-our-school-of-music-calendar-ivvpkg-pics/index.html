Embark on a Musical Odyssey: Our School of Music Calendar

Music, an art form that transcends language and culture, has the power to inspire, evoke emotions, and connect people from all walks of life. Our School of Music is proud to present an extraordinary calendar of events that will captivate audiences with a diverse array of performances, workshops, and masterclasses. From classical symphonies to contemporary jazz ensembles, from intimate recitals to grand-scale operas, our calendar offers something for every music lover.

A Symphony of Sounds

Our season kicks off with a breathtaking performance by the renowned Boston Symphony Orchestra. Conducted by the esteemed Andris Nelsons, the orchestra will present a program of beloved classics, including Beethoven’s Symphony No. 5 and Tchaikovsky’s Romeo and Juliet Overture. The evening promises an unforgettable experience, showcasing the virtuosity and artistry of one of the world’s finest ensembles.

For lovers of chamber music, we present the Emerson String Quartet. This Grammy Award-winning ensemble will perform a captivating program featuring works by Haydn, Mozart, and Beethoven. Their impeccable intonation, nuanced interpretations, and seamless collaboration will transport audiences to a realm of musical bliss.

Jazz Giants Take the Stage

The rhythms of jazz will ignite our stage with performances by two legendary artists. Jazz icon Wynton Marsalis will lead his renowned septet in an electrifying evening of bebop, swing, and ballads. His trumpet playing, known for its technical brilliance and emotional depth, will leave audiences spellbound.

Acclaimed pianist Keith Jarrett will grace our stage for an intimate solo performance. Jarrett’s improvisational genius and ability to create spontaneous masterpieces have made him one of the most revered jazz musicians of our time. Prepare for an evening of musical exploration and discovery.

Vocal Virtuosity

Our calendar features a showcase of vocal excellence, beginning with a performance by the Metropolitan Opera. This world-renowned opera company will present a breathtaking production of Verdi’s La Traviata, featuring a stellar cast of singers and a stunning orchestra. The passion, drama, and vocal fireworks of this timeless masterpiece will captivate audiences.

For a more intimate vocal experience, we present a recital by the celebrated soprano Renée Fleming. Her exquisite voice, impeccable technique, and profound interpretations have made her one of the most beloved opera singers of our time. Fleming’s program will include arias from Puccini, Verdi, and Mozart, promising an evening of vocal splendor.

Workshops and Masterclasses

Beyond performances, our calendar offers a wealth of educational opportunities. Renowned musicians will lead workshops and masterclasses, providing students and aspiring musicians with invaluable insights and guidance.

The International Piano Festival will bring together a world-class faculty of pianists, including Lang Lang, Emanuel Ax, and Mitsuko Uchida. Participants will have the opportunity to learn from these masters, participate in masterclasses, and showcase their own talents in recitals.

The Young Musicians’ Academy offers a transformative summer program for talented young musicians. Students will receive intensive instruction in their instruments, participate in chamber ensembles, and perform in public concerts. This program provides an exceptional opportunity for young musicians to develop their skills and explore their musical potential.

Community Outreach

Our School of Music is committed to enriching the lives of our community through music. We offer a variety of outreach programs, including free concerts, educational workshops, and instrument lessons for underprivileged youth.

The Community Music Project brings music education to underserved communities. Through partnerships with local schools and community centers, we provide free music lessons, instruments, and performance opportunities to children who might not otherwise have access to music education.

A Legacy of Excellence

For over a century, our School of Music has been a beacon of musical excellence. Our faculty consists of internationally renowned musicians, scholars, and educators who are dedicated to nurturing the talents of our students and inspiring our audiences.

Our state-of-the-art performance venues, including the historic Meany Hall and the intimate Nordstrom Recital Hall, provide an exceptional setting for our performances. Our acoustically perfect halls allow musicians to showcase their artistry and audiences to experience the full spectrum of musical expression.

Join Us for an Unforgettable Musical Journey

Whether you are a seasoned music lover, a budding musician, or simply someone who appreciates the beauty of music, our School of Music calendar offers an unparalleled opportunity to experience the transformative power of music.

Visit our website or contact our box office for tickets and more information. Join us for an unforgettable musical journey that will leave a lasting impression on your heart and soul.

Calendar Highlights:

  • Emerson String Quartet: November 10
  • Wynton Marsalis Septet: January 15
  • Keith Jarrett Solo Piano: February 22
  • Metropolitan Opera: La Traviata: March 10-15
  • Renée Fleming Recital: April 12
  • International Piano Festival: June 1-15
  • Young Musicians’ Academy: July 1-28
  • Community Music Project: Ongoing throughout the year
